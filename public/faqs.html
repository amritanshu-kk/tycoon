Question:

Can we pre-train our models?

Answer:

Participants will not be allowed to submit a pre-trained model. Submitting pre-trained models violates the spirit of this challenge. We will deploy your code on a centralized server and evaluate your models on a set of evaluation instances that will not be shared with you in advance. This ensures that results can be properly reproduced by us and that no participant team has an unfair advantage due to computing resources. We may also re-train your models on a slightly different dataset than the one that was provided to the participants. Finalist submissions will also undergo an in-depth code review by our evaluation team to ensure that nobody has an unfair advantage, e.g., from pre-training their model and disguising it in their submission.

Question:

How are stops anonymized in the data?

Answer:

Locations are anonymized only if a particular household's purchasing behavior might be inferred from the data. The coordinates of package dropoff locations, if anonymized, are mapped deterministically to a new location. Travel times are historically realized and thus determined by the customer's actual location. Note that a stop's location is only minorly modified, so changes in transit times should be minimal.

Question:

How do time windows affect route scores?

Answer:

The scoring algorithm is not affected by time windows. However, since the goal of your model is to match high-quality routes and high-quality routes are likely to meet time windows, missing time windows will probably have a negative impact on your score. In this sense, both being early and late will decrease a route's score.

Question:

Should drivers wait between stops in order to meet the time window for package delivery if they would reach a stop before the package time window starts?

Answer:

This is a possibility. You can design your model to assume this if you desire. However, this is in no way a foregone conclusion. Remember that your goal is to match high-quality routes, not meet time windows. While part of what makes a route high-quality is meeting time windows, we do not guarantee that every high-quality route meets every time window.

Question:

To meet the time window, when is a package considered delivered? Is it after arriving at the stop or after the service time?

Answer:

The time the delivery person finishes serving a particular package is the time that should be used to determine whether the time window is met. Arriving at the package's corresponding stop does not mean that the package has been delivered.

Question:

In the dataset, there are multiple stops (ex., VE, SP, EA, ...) with the same latitude and longitude. What is the reason for this?

Answer:

There are three possible reasons for this. First, multiple stops might share the same physical location, such as an apartment building. Also, for privacy purposes, the given coordinates are not the exact coordinates of the stops. The process of altering coordinates may have resulted in some nearby stops being assigned the same coordinates. Finally, Stop IDs are only unique within each route. This means that a stop that is found in multiple routes probably has a different ID in each route.

Question:

What defines an infeasible route sequence?

Answer:

"There are only two reasons for a sequence to be invalid: 1) it does not start at the delivery station 2) it does not contain all the stops of the route (and only the stops of that route of course) This means, time window violations will NOT result in the sequence being treated as invalid. However, keep in mind that a sequence that significantly violates time windows is unlikely to be close to its high-quality benchmark sequence."
